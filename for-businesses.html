Why getting certified by ClaimCheck

Two problems solved. One certification.

Most manufacturers think about health claims as a marketing decision. There are also a reputational and regulatory one.

Consumer credibility

A visible ClaimCheck logo signals to consumers and retailers that your health claims have been scrutinized by an independent body—and held up. In a category where trust is low and skepticism is high, that signal is a genuine differentiator.

Getting your health claims backed by science and certified by an independent body directly increases the visibility and credibility of your brand. As consumer scrutiny around the science behind health claims continues to grow, certified brands will stand out—and stay ahead.

Regulatory protection

Manufacturers who certify with ClaimCheck receive a full regulatory compliance report alongside their scientific validity rating. Our evaluation explicitly assesses whether your health claim complies with FDA structure/function requirements under DSHEA.

Brands with a documented, independent review of health claim compliance are significantly better positioned in the event of an FDA inquiry or warning letter. You will have evidence of due diligence that most manufacturers simply do not have.

The Process

Improve the credibility of your product

The ClaimCheck evaluation is rigorous but straightforward. We handle the scientific and regulatory review, you get a clear report and, when warranted, a certification logo you can display.

1

Submit your claim and product

Provide the exact label claim text, the ingredient(s) in question, dosage information, and any existing substantiation you have. We work with the claim as it appears on your packaging.

2

Two-axis review

Our panel conducts a dual evaluation: regulatory compliance (does the claim meet DSHEA structure/function requirements?) and scientific validity (does peer-reviewed literature support it at the stated dose and mechanism?).

3

Full evaluation report

You receive a detailed written report with a scientific accuracy score from 0 to 100, a label grade from A to C reflecting the strength of evidence, the supporting or contradicting literature, our regulatory compliance assessment, and specific recommendations if applicable.

4

Display your certification

Green-rated claims qualify for the ClaimCheck certification logo. Display it on your packaging, website, and marketing materials. Your rating is also published to our public database, and shared with our community of health influencers.

Supporting Scientific Literature

“Supports immune health” — Vitamin C 500mg

Carr & Maggini — Nutrients, 2017 94%

Most cited modern review. Vitamin C enhances neutrophil chemotaxis, phagocytosis, and epithelial barrier function. Prophylactic intake maintains immune readiness; higher doses required during active infection.

Hemilä & Chalker — Cochrane Database, 2013 88%

Gold-standard meta-analysis. ~50% reduction in cold incidence in high-stress populations (marathon runners, soldiers). Modest reduction in duration across the general population.

Wintergerst, Maggini & Hornig — Annals of Nutrition & Metabolism, 2006 81%

Vitamin C plasma levels decline during infection and stress. Supplementation improved NK cell activity, lymphocyte proliferation, antimicrobial function, and delayed-type hypersensitivity.

Hemilä — Nutrients, 2017 76%

Low vitamin C status significantly increases respiratory infection risk. Prophylactic supplementation reduces risk of respiratory viruses, particularly under physical or environmental stress.

Orange to Green

Got an Orange? We can help you get to Green.

An Orange rating means the science is mixed, limited, or inconclusive—not that there is no path forward. Many Orange health claims can be improved through reformulation, dosage adjustment, or more precise health claim language that more accurately reflects what the evidence does support.

Our consulting team works with manufacturers after an Orange rating to identify the specific gap between current evidence and a Green outcome. We review the literature in detail, identify what formulation or claim changes would close the gap, and help you arrive at a stronger, scientifically credible position.

The goal is not to game the rating system. It is to help you make a genuinely better product—one that delivers what the label says. That is better for consumers, better for your brand, and better for the industry.
