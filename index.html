The Regulatory Gap

The FDA doesn’t check.
Neither does anyone else.

The supplement industry operates under a regulatory framework that requires almost nothing from manufacturers before a product reaches store shelves.

Manufacturers self-certify

Under DSHEA, any manufacturer can make a structure/function claim with no prior FDA review, no approval process, and near-zero risk of enforcement. The bar for putting a health claim on a label is effectively self-attestation.

The disclaimer means nothing

That warning label consumers see on every supplement—“This statement has not been evaluated by the FDA”—is not a formality. It is an admission that no one has verified the claim is true. It is there because the law requires it, not because it signals anything has been checked.

Legal ≠ Scientific

The FDA evaluates claim format, not claim truth. “Calcium supports bowel regularity” is as legally valid as “Fiber supports bowel regularity.” The science tells a completely different story. Legal permissibility and scientific validity are not the same standard.

Our Approach

Independent scientific validation. Two dimensions. One clear signal.

ClaimCheck evaluates every claim on two independent axes. The first is regulatory compliance—is the claim legally structured correctly under DSHEA? The second is scientific validity—does the peer-reviewed evidence actually support what the claim says?

A claim can pass one axis and fail the other. Most databases only check one. We assess both—and we publish every result, regardless of whether a manufacturer engaged with us.

See How Certification Works

GREEN — Strong Evidence

Strong, consistent, replicated scientific evidence supports the claim. The mechanism is well understood and backed by multiple high-quality studies.

ORANGE — Limited Evidence

Limited, mixed, or low-quality evidence. Some research supports the claim, but the science is inconclusive, preliminary, or methodologically weak.

RED — No Credible Evidence

No meaningful scientific evidence supports the claim. The ingredient, dose, or mechanism is not supported by credible, peer-reviewed research.

Real Evaluations

See it in action

These are the kinds of claims ClaimCheck evaluates every day—some well-supported, some not.

Vitamin C

“Supports immune health”

GREEN — Strong Evidence

Extensive, replicated evidence supports Vitamin C’s role in immune function. Multiple systematic reviews and large clinical trials confirm its role in supporting immune cell activity and reducing oxidative stress.

Omega-3 Fatty Acids

“Promotes cardiovascular health”

GREEN — Strong Evidence

Strong evidence from multiple large-scale trials supports this claim. Omega-3s have a well-established mechanism for reducing triglycerides and inflammation, with consistent results across diverse patient populations.

Calcium

“Supports bowel regularity”

RED — No Credible Evidence

No meaningful scientific evidence supports calcium as a driver of bowel regularity. This claim is legally permissible—it meets the DSHEA structure/function format—but it is not supported by the scientific literature.

Proprietary Herbal Blend

“Boosts energy levels”

RED — No Credible Evidence

A vague claim with no defined mechanism and no credible supporting evidence. “Proprietary blend” obscures dosage information, making independent evaluation impossible. The claim cannot be substantiated.
